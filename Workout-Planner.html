Biceps Exercises

Warm-up

Begin with 5-10 minutes of light cardio, such as arm circles, brisk walking, or light jogging. Follow with dynamic stretches like arm swings, and torso twists to prepare your biceps and upper body for the workout.

  • Dumbbell Bicep Curls

    Stand with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing forward. Curl the weights up to your shoulders, keeping your elbows still. Lower slowly. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Hammer Curls

    Hold dumbbells with palms facing each other. Curl the weights up, keeping palms facing inwards throughout. Lower with control. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Concentration Curls

    Sit on a bench, legs wider than shoulder-width, and lean forward. Place the back of your upper right arm against the inner right thigh. Curl the dumbbell up, focusing on your bicep. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15 (each arm). Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Cable Curls

    Attach a straight bar to a low cable pulley. Stand facing the machine, shoulder-width apart. Curl the bar upwards towards your shoulders, keeping elbows close to your body. Lower slowly. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Incline Dumbbell Curls

    Sit on an incline bench with a dumbbell in each hand, arms hanging down, palms facing forward. Curl the weights up, maintaining your upper arm position. Lower slowly. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

Cool-down

Finish your biceps workout with 5-10 minutes of static stretching. Focus on stretching your biceps and forearms. Examples include bicep stretch against a wall and forearm extensor stretch. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds.

Triceps Exercises

Warm-up

Start with 5-10 minutes of light cardio like jumping jacks or arm rotations to increase blood flow. Include dynamic stretches such as arm circles, and triceps stretches to prepare your triceps and upper body.

  • Triceps Dips (Bench Dips)

    Use a bench or chair. Place your hands shoulder-width apart on the edge, fingers pointing forward. Extend your legs out. Lower your body by bending your elbows, then push back up. Sets: 3, Reps: As many as possible (AMRAP). Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Close-Grip Bench Press

    Lie on a bench, grip the barbell with hands closer than shoulder-width. Lower the bar to your chest, keeping elbows tucked in. Push the bar back up. Sets: 3, Reps: 8-10. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Overhead Triceps Extension (Dumbbell)

    Hold a dumbbell with both hands and extend it overhead. Lower the weight behind your head by bending your elbows, then extend back up. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Cable Pushdowns

    Attach a rope handle to a high cable pulley. Grip the rope with palms facing each other. Keeping elbows tucked in, push the rope down until your arms are fully extended. Control the weight back up. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Skullcrushers (Lying Triceps Extensions)

    Lie on a bench holding a barbell or dumbbells with arms extended above you. Keeping upper arms stationary, lower the weight towards your forehead by bending your elbows. Extend back up. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

Cool-down

Conclude with 5-10 minutes of static stretches focusing on your triceps and shoulders. Try overhead triceps stretch and cross-body shoulder stretch, holding each for 20-30 seconds to improve flexibility and reduce muscle tension.

Chest Exercises

Warm-up

Warm up for 5-10 minutes with light cardio such as jumping jacks or arm rotations. Perform dynamic chest stretches like arm swings, and chest wall stretch to prepare your chest muscles for exercise.

  • Bench Press (Barbell or Dumbbell)

    Lie on a bench. For barbell, grip slightly wider than shoulder-width. For dumbbells, hold in each hand. Lower weight to chest, then push back up. Sets: 3, Reps: 8-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Push-Ups (Classic or Incline)

    Classic push-ups: Hands shoulder-width apart, body straight. Lower chest to the floor, push back up. Incline push-ups (easier): Hands on elevated surface. Sets: 3, Reps: As many as possible (AMRAP). Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Dumbbell Flyes

    Lie on a bench with dumbbells in each hand, arms extended above chest, palms facing each other. Lower arms out to the sides in an arc, keeping a slight bend in elbows. Bring weights back up to starting position. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Incline Dumbbell Press

    Lie on an incline bench with dumbbells. Lower weights to your upper chest, then press back up. Sets: 3, Reps: 8-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Cable Crossovers

    Stand in the center of a cable crossover machine. Grip the handles of high pulleys. Keeping a slight bend in your elbows, bring your hands together in front of you in a wide arc. Control the return. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

Cool-down

Cool down with 5-10 minutes of static chest and shoulder stretches. Pectoral stretch and doorway chest stretch are effective. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds to help muscle recovery.

Back Exercises

Warm-up

Begin with 5-10 minutes of light cardio like rowing or jumping jacks to get your blood flowing. Incorporate dynamic back stretches such as cat-cow stretches and torso rotations to prepare your back muscles.

  • Pull-Ups (or Lat Pulldowns)

    Pull-ups (advanced): Grip a pull-up bar with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width. Hang and pull yourself up until your chin is over the bar. Lat pulldowns (beginner-friendly): Use a lat pulldown machine, pull the bar down to your chest. Sets: 3, Reps: As many as possible (AMRAP) for Pull-ups, 10-12 for Lat pulldowns. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Bent-Over Rows (Barbell or Dumbbell)

    Bent-Over Barbell Rows: Bend at the hips, keeping back straight, and row a barbell towards your lower chest. Dumbbell Rows: Support yourself on a bench with one hand and knee, row a dumbbell with the other arm. Sets: 3, Reps: 8-12 (each arm for dumbbell rows). Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Seated Cable Rows

    Sit at a seated cable row machine, feet against the rests, knees slightly bent. Pull the handle towards your torso, squeezing your back muscles. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Lat Pulldowns

    Use a lat pulldown machine, sit with knees under the pads. Grip the bar with a wide overhand grip and pull it down to your chest, squeezing your back muscles. Control the bar back up. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Back Extensions (Hyperextensions)

    Use a back extension bench. Position yourself with hips on the pad, legs secured. Lower your torso towards the floor, keeping your back straight. Extend back up, engaging your lower back muscles. Sets: 3, Reps: 15-20. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

Cool-down

Finish with 5-10 minutes of static back stretches like child’s pose and seated back twist. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds to promote back muscle recovery and flexibility.

Shoulder Exercises

Warm-up

Warm up for 5-10 minutes with light cardio, focusing on arm and shoulder movements such as arm circles and torso twists. Include dynamic shoulder stretches like arm swings and shoulder rotations to prepare your shoulder muscles.

  • Overhead Press (Barbell or Dumbbell)

    Stand or sit, and press a barbell or dumbbells straight overhead. Lower with control to shoulder level. Sets: 3, Reps: 8-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Lateral Raises

    Stand with dumbbells in hand, arms at your sides. Raise your arms out to the sides until shoulder level, keeping a slight bend in elbows. Lower slowly. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Front Raises

    Stand with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing your thighs. Raise one or both arms straight in front of you until shoulder level. Lower slowly. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Rear Delt Flyes (Reverse Dumbbell Flyes)

    Bend forward at the hips, keeping back straight, with dumbbells in each hand, palms facing each other. Raise your arms out to the sides, squeezing your rear deltoids. Lower with control. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Face Pulls

    Attach a rope handle to a high cable pulley. Grip the ends of the rope with an overhand grip. Pull the rope towards your face, separating your hands as you pull and externally rotating your shoulders. Sets: 3, Reps: 15-20. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

Cool-down

Cool down with 5-10 minutes of static shoulder stretches. Cross-body shoulder stretch and overhead triceps stretch will help in shoulder muscle recovery. Hold each for 20-30 seconds.

Leg Exercises

Warm-up

Start with 5-10 minutes of light cardio such as brisk walking or high knees. Include dynamic leg stretches like leg swings, hip circles, and torso twists to prepare your leg muscles and joints.

  • Squats (Bodyweight or Barbell)

    Bodyweight squats: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, squat down as if sitting in a chair, keeping back straight. Barbell squats (advanced): Place a barbell across your upper back, squat down maintaining form. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Lunges (Forward or Walking)

    Forward lunges: Step forward with one leg, bending both knees to 90 degrees. Push back to starting position and repeat on the other leg. Walking lunges: Step forward and lunge, then step through with the back leg into another lunge, alternating legs as you walk. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-12 (each leg). Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Calf Raises (Standing or Seated)

    Standing calf raises: Stand on a slightly elevated surface (like a step), heels hanging off. Rise up onto your toes, feeling the stretch in your calves. Seated calf raises: Sit with weight on your thighs, raise up onto your toes. Sets: 3, Reps: 15-20. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Leg Press

    Using a leg press machine, place your feet shoulder-width apart on the platform. Press the platform away from you, extending your legs, but without locking your knees. Lower slowly. Sets: 3, Reps: 10-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Hamstring Curls (Leg Curls)

    Using a hamstring curl machine (lying or seated), curl your legs up towards your glutes, flexing your hamstrings. Control the weight back down. Sets: 3, Reps: 12-15. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

Cool-down

Cool down with 5-10 minutes of static leg stretches, including quadriceps stretch, hamstring stretch, and calf stretch. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds to improve leg muscle flexibility and aid recovery.

Abs Exercises

Warm-up

Warm up for 5-10 minutes with light cardio like torso twists and brisk walking. Include dynamic core stretches such as hip circles and cat-cow stretches to prepare your core muscles for the workout.

  • Crunches

    Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Place hands behind your head or across your chest. Curl your upper body towards your knees, lifting your shoulders off the floor. Lower slowly. Sets: 3, Reps: 15-20. Rest: 30-45 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Leg Raises

    Lie flat on your back, legs extended. Place hands under your glutes for support. Lift your legs straight up towards the ceiling, keeping them straight or slightly bent. Lower slowly. Sets: 3, Reps: 15-20. Rest: 30-45 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Plank

    Start in a push-up position, but rest on your forearms instead of hands. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core. Hold for time. Sets: 3, Hold: 30-60 seconds. Rest: 30-45 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Russian Twists

    Sit on the floor with knees bent, feet slightly off the ground, and lean back slightly. Hold a weight or medicine ball (optional). Twist your torso from side to side, touching the weight (or hands) to the floor on each side. Sets: 3, Reps: 15-20 (each side). Rest: 30-45 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Bicycle Crunches

    Lie on your back with hands behind your head. Bring knees towards your chest and alternate bringing your elbow to the opposite knee while extending the other leg straight out. Sets: 3, Reps: 15-20 (each side). Rest: 30-45 seconds between sets.

    Ready

Cool-down

Cool down with 5-10 minutes of static core stretches like cobra stretch and side stretches. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds to relax your core muscles and improve flexibility.

Full Body Workout

This full body routine targets major muscle groups in each workout. It's efficient for overall fitness and can be done 2-3 times a week with rest days.

Warm-up

Begin with a 5-10 minute full body warm-up. Start with light cardio such as jogging in place or jumping jacks, followed by dynamic stretches like arm circles, leg swings, and torso rotations to prepare your entire body.

  • Squats

    Sets: 3, Reps: 10-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Push-Ups

    Sets: 3, Reps: As many as possible (AMRAP). Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Bent-Over Rows

    Sets: 3, Reps: 8-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Overhead Press

    Sets: 3, Reps: 8-12. Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Plank

    Sets: 3, Hold: 30-60 seconds. Rest: 30-45 seconds between sets.

    Ready

  • Crunches

    Sets: 3, Reps: 15-20. Rest: 30-45 seconds between sets.

    Ready

Cool-down

Conclude with a 5-10 minute full body cool-down, incorporating static stretches for major muscle groups worked, such as quadriceps, hamstrings, back, chest, and shoulders. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds.

Important Workout Notes:

  • Warm-up: Always begin with the recommended warm-up before each workout to prepare your muscles and joints.
  • Cool-down: Finish with the suggested cool-down stretches to improve flexibility and aid muscle recovery.
  • Proper Form: Focus on maintaining correct form over lifting heavy weight to prevent injuries. Watch videos and/or consult a trainer to learn proper technique for each exercise. Images are placeholders. Refer to reliable fitness resources for correct form before adding weight.
  • Rest: Adhere to the recommended rest times between sets to optimize muscle recovery and workout effectiveness.
  • Progression: As you get stronger, you can increase the weight, reps, or sets.
  • Listen to your body: If you feel pain, stop the exercise.
  • Workout Frequency: For muscle growth, aim to workout each muscle group 2-3 times per week with rest days in between.
  • Customize: Adjust sets, reps, exercises, warm-up, and cool-down based on your fitness level, goals, and preferences. This is a sample plan, feel free to modify it.
